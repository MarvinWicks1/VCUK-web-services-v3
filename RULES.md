# VCUK Website Redesign Rules

These rules protect the VCUK site from becoming generic, overdesigned, unclear, or untrustworthy.

## Brand Rules

- VCUK must feel premium, practical, and human.
- VCUK must not pretend to be a large agency.
- The site should make Ian feel accessible and credible.
- Use direct language that a small business owner understands.
- Every page should answer: "How does this help a business get clearer, trusted, and easier to contact?"

## Copy Rules

- Write in plain English.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- Use specific examples from real local businesses.
- Make pricing, process, and next steps clear.
- Say "from £350" only with scope context.
- Use "Ian" where personal trust helps.

Do not use:

- Generic agency filler.
- AI-sounding phrases.
- Fake urgency.
- Overpromising SEO results.
- Claims that cannot be proved.

Banned or discouraged phrases:

- Unlock your potential.
- Take your business to the next level.
- Bespoke digital solutions.
- Stunning websites.
- We are passionate about.
- Seamless experience.
- Cutting-edge solutions.
- Skyrocket your traffic.
- Guaranteed rankings.

Preferred phrases:

- Make enquiries easier.
- One clear place for customers to understand what you do.
- Clear services, proof, and contact routes.
- Designed around how customers choose.
- Practical SEO foundations.
- No confusing tech talk.
- Direct with Ian.

## Design Rules

- The site must look custom, not like a theme.
- Do not rely on endless card grids.
- Do not use decorative blobs, random gradients, or generic tech visuals.
- Use real or realistic website visuals, screenshots, proof panels, and service-business examples.
- Keep mobile layouts tight, readable, and conversion-focused.
- CTAs must be easy to find but not shouty.
- Avoid large blocks of centered text.
- Avoid low-contrast grey text.
- Avoid stock images of laptops, handshakes, boardrooms, or generic agency teams.

## Proof Rules

- Real work must be real.
- Concept work must be clearly labelled as concept/example work.
- Testimonials must only be used with permission.
- Do not imply client outcomes that are not proven.
- Show process and thinking when portfolio depth is limited.

## SEO Rules

- One H1 per page.
- Unique title and meta description for every page.
- Use natural local wording.
- Do not keyword stuff.
- Add internal links between guides, services, pricing, and contact.
- Optimise images before launch.
- Add alt text to meaningful images.
- Include sitemap and robots.txt.
- Add schema only when the details are accurate.

## Conversion Rules

- Every main page needs a primary CTA above the fold.
- Every main page needs a final CTA.
- Pricing must be easy to find.
- Contact options must be tappable on mobile.
- Forms must have success and error states.
- Do not use `mailto:` as the only form method.
- The site should support outreach by making VCUK look credible when prospects check it.

## Performance Rules

- Keep JavaScript minimal.
- Avoid animation libraries unless there is a clear reason.
- Optimise images and use responsive sizes.
- Avoid layout shift.
- Test on mobile, not just desktop.
- Target 90+ Lighthouse performance on mobile.

## Accessibility Rules

- Use semantic HTML.
- All form inputs need labels.
- Buttons and links need visible focus states.
- Text must meet contrast requirements.
- Navigation must work by keyboard.
- Do not hide important text inside images.

## Commercial Rules

- VCUK is selling clarity, trust, and enquiries, not just a website.
- The one-page website offer must feel focused, not cheap.
- Scope must be clear before build.
- Avoid promising that a website alone will fix sales.
- Make ownership, hosting, support, and ongoing costs clear.

## Final Quality Bar

Before calling the redesign ready, ask:

- Does it look like a web designer worth hiring?
- Does it feel specific to VCUK?
- Can a cold outreach prospect trust it within 30 seconds?
- Is pricing clear without making the brand feel cheap?
- Is there proof or labelled example work?
- Is the mobile experience excellent?
- Does the copy sound like Ian, not a template?

If the answer to any of these is no, keep refining.
