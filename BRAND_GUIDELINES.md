# VCUK Web Services Brand Guidelines

These guidelines define the visual and verbal brand system for the VCUK Web Services redesign. They are based on the current VCUK logo and Facebook post style: clean white space, deep navy, bright blue, soft blue-grey accents, bold typography, rounded UI, and practical small-business messaging.

## Brand Summary

VCUK should feel like a premium, practical web service for small local businesses.

The brand is:

- Clear.
- Confident.
- Technical enough to feel capable.
- Human enough to feel approachable.
- Premium without becoming corporate.
- Local without feeling small or basic.

The website should look like it was made by someone who understands structure, trust, conversion, and visual polish.

## Core Brand Idea

> Clear websites, practical next steps.

Supporting message:

> VCUK helps small businesses turn scattered online information into clear, professional websites that make services, proof, and contact routes easy to find.

This should guide the whole site. VCUK is not selling decoration. VCUK is selling clarity, trust, and better enquiry journeys.

## Logo

### Primary Logo

Use the full VCUK Web Services logo on white or very light backgrounds wherever possible.

The logo uses:

- Deep navy lettering.
- Bright blue accent mark.
- Thin blue lines around "Web Services".
- A modern technical mark that suggests structure, build quality, and digital confidence.

### Logo Usage Rules

Use:

- Full logo in the header.
- Full logo in footer if space allows.
- Icon/mark only for favicon, social avatar, small badges, or compact UI.
- White or very light backgrounds behind the logo.

Avoid:

- Placing the logo on busy images.
- Stretching or squashing the logo.
- Recolouring the logo.
- Adding shadows, gradients, glows, or outlines to the logo.
- Using the logo too small for "Web Services" to remain legible.

### Logo Clear Space

Keep clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of the VCUK icon mark. In tight mobile headers, preserve enough breathing room so the logo does not feel crammed against navigation or CTA buttons.

## Colour Palette

The colour system should stay close to the existing Facebook post and logo style.

### Primary Colours

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Primary dark | VCUK Navy | `#071B3A` | Headlines, logo text, footer, strong contrast sections |
| Primary accent | VCUK Blue | `#006BCB` | Buttons, links, highlights, icon circles, active states |
| Accent dark | Deep Blue | `#004F9F` | Button hover, deep accent panels, emphasis |
| Page background | Clean White | `#FFFFFF` | Main page background |
| Soft background | Frost Blue | `#F5F8FC` | Alternating sections, card backgrounds, subtle panels |
| Pale accent | Soft Blue Grey | `#E7EEF8` | Pills, badges, dividers, decorative UI shapes |

### Neutral Colours

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Body text | Ink Text | `#101827` | Paragraphs and UI text |
| Secondary text | Slate Text | `#526071` | Supporting copy, meta text, captions |
| Border | Light Border | `#D8E2EF` | Cards, form fields, separators |
| Muted border | Pale Border | `#EEF3F8` | Large soft sections and decorative lines |
| Surface | Card White | `#FFFFFF` | Cards, forms, laptop UI, content panels |

### Colour Ratio

Recommended visual balance:

- 70% white / off-white space.
- 20% deep navy and dark text.
- 8% VCUK blue.
- 2% soft decorative blue-grey details.

The blue should feel intentional and premium, not sprayed everywhere.

## Colour Usage Rules

Use VCUK Blue for:

- Primary CTAs.
- Important links.
- Active navigation states.
- Small icons.
- Badge backgrounds.
- Underline accents.
- Circular callout elements.

Use VCUK Navy for:

- Headlines.
- Strong section titles.
- Footer background.
- High-trust proof sections.
- Text-heavy authority areas.

Use Frost Blue for:

- Section backgrounds.
- Checker/tool areas.
- Pricing comparison zones.
- Guide card backgrounds.
- Soft visual separation.

Avoid:

- Purple gradients.
- Beige/tan palettes.
- Dark slate SaaS styling.
- Neon blues.
- Too many blues competing together.
- Low-contrast pale blue text.
- Black-heavy design that loses the clean VCUK feel.

## Typography

The brand currently uses bold, modern sans-serif typography. The website should keep that feeling.

### Recommended Font Direction

Use one strong sans-serif family with multiple weights, or pair a geometric display sans with a highly readable body sans.

Recommended options:

- `Inter`
- `Manrope`
- `Plus Jakarta Sans`
- `Sora`
- `Outfit`

Preferred practical pairing:

- Headings: `Manrope` or `Sora`
- Body/UI: `Inter`

If performance is the priority, use one font family only.

### Typography Style

Headlines should be:

- Bold.
- Clean.
- Confident.
- Slightly technical.
- Easy to read on mobile.

Body copy should be:

- Plain.
- Spacious.
- Direct.
- Written for business owners, not developers.

### Suggested Type Scale

Desktop:

- Hero H1: 56-72px, bold, tight but readable line height.
- Page H1: 48-60px.
- Section H2: 34-44px.
- Card H3: 22-28px.
- Body: 17-19px.
- Small/meta: 13-15px.

Mobile:

- Hero H1: 38-46px.
- Page H1: 34-42px.
- Section H2: 28-34px.
- Body: 16-18px.

Avoid:

- Tiny body text.
- Overly thin heading weights.
- Negative letter spacing.
- All-caps paragraphs.
- Huge hero text that hides the CTA below the fold.

## Layout Style

The layout should reflect the Facebook post style:

- Clean white space.
- Strong centered or left-aligned headlines.
- Blue accent moments.
- Rounded pills.
- Soft background shapes.
- Device/browser previews.
- Practical UI mockups.

For the website, avoid making every section a centered social graphic. The site should mature the social style into a premium web experience.

### Layout Rules

Use:

- Full-width sections with controlled max-width content.
- Strong hero composition with a realistic website preview.
- Alternating white and Frost Blue sections.
- Editorial split sections for explanation.
- Large proof visuals.
- Practical tool/checker UI.
- Clean pricing comparison.

Avoid:

- Endless equal-height cards.
- Overlapping decorative shapes that hurt readability.
- Huge empty hero with no proof.
- Generic agency grids.
- Decorative visuals that do not explain anything.

## UI Components

### Buttons

Primary button:

- Background: `#006BCB`
- Text: white.
- Hover: `#004F9F`
- Border radius: 10-14px.
- Weight: 700.
- Feel: confident, clear, tappable.

Secondary button:

- White or transparent background.
- Navy text.
- Light blue-grey border.
- Hover with Frost Blue background.

Avoid:

- Pill buttons everywhere.
- Tiny buttons.
- Low-contrast outline buttons.
- Gradient buttons.

### Pills and Badges

Use rounded pills for short labels such as:

- New tool added.
- Websites from £350.
- Direct with Ian.
- Winsford, Cheshire.
- Mobile-first.

Pill styling:

- Background: `#E7EEF8`
- Text: `#004F9F` or `#071B3A`
- Radius: 999px.
- Font: bold, 13-15px.
- Letter spacing: normal or very slight.

### Cards and Panels

Cards should feel clean and useful, not decorative.

Use:

- White background.
- Light border.
- Subtle shadow only where needed.
- Radius: 12-18px for major UI panels.
- Radius: 8-12px for smaller cards.

Avoid:

- Heavy shadows.
- Glassmorphism.
- Nested cards.
- Cards with no meaningful content.

### Forms

Forms should match the laptop mockup style from the social post:

- White panels.
- Light border.
- Rounded inputs.
- Clear labels.
- Strong blue submit button.
- Helpful but minimal placeholder text.

Inputs:

- Border: `#D8E2EF`
- Focus border: `#006BCB`
- Background: white.
- Radius: 8-10px.

## Decorative System

The Facebook post uses light tech details that should carry into the website carefully.

Allowed decorative elements:

- Light dotted grids.
- Thin line accents.
- Soft geometric corner shapes.
- Subtle hexagon outlines.
- Blue underline strokes.
- Circular blue callouts.
- Browser/device frames.

Rules:

- Decorations must stay pale and secondary.
- Decorations must not distract from copy.
- Do not use more than one or two decorative systems per section.
- Decorative elements should support a "digital clarity" feel.

Avoid:

- Random abstract blobs.
- Heavy gradients.
- Floating orbs.
- Excessive animation.
- Decorative elements that look AI-generated.

## Imagery and Mockups

The brand should rely on inspectable, relevant visuals:

- Website screenshots.
- Browser frames.
- Mobile/desktop previews.
- Form/checker UI.
- Case-study visuals.
- Before/after structure examples.
- Real client or concept site mockups.

Avoid:

- Generic laptop stock photos.
- Handshake photos.
- Boardroom photos.
- Random people in offices.
- Abstract AI art.
- Visuals from unrelated industries.

If a concept build is shown, label it clearly as an example build.

## Social Post Style

The Facebook post style should remain part of the brand system.

Post structure:

- Logo at top.
- Small rounded badge.
- Large bold headline.
- Navy and blue emphasis.
- Short practical subheading.
- Central website/device visual.
- Circular blue callout.
- Clear URL or CTA strip.

The website should borrow this structure for campaign sections and tool pages, but with more web-native spacing and less poster-like density.

## Voice and Messaging

The brand voice is:

- Straightforward.
- Practical.
- Helpful.
- Commercially clear.
- Calmly confident.

Use wording like:

- "Quick checks. Practical next steps."
- "Clear services, proof, and contact routes."
- "One clear place for customers to understand what you do."
- "Websites designed around how customers choose."
- "No confusing tech talk."
- "Direct with Ian."

Avoid wording like:

- "Revolutionise your online presence."
- "Unlock limitless growth."
- "We craft bespoke digital experiences."
- "Skyrocket your business."
- "Guaranteed Google rankings."

## Website-Specific Brand Rules

The website must:

- Feel more premium than the Facebook post while clearly belonging to the same brand.
- Use the same blue/navy/white language.
- Keep strong headline hierarchy.
- Use device and website visuals to show capability.
- Make pricing and contact routes obvious.
- Show practical proof, not vague claims.

The website must not:

- Look like a generic agency template.
- Abandon the VCUK blue/white identity.
- Become too dark.
- Overuse social-post style graphics on every section.
- Hide behind abstract language.

## Example Homepage Visual Direction

Hero composition:

- White or Frost Blue background.
- Logo/header above.
- Left side: headline, short copy, CTA buttons, trust strip.
- Right side: premium browser/laptop preview of a VCUK-built local business website.
- Subtle dotted grid or geometric accent in the background.
- Blue accent line or pill to connect with social post styling.

Example headline treatment:

> Clearer websites for local businesses that need more enquiries, not more confusion.

Use navy for most of the headline and VCUK Blue for one meaningful phrase, such as:

- clearer websites
- more enquiries
- one clear place

Do not colour every other word.

## Accessibility and Contrast

Use high contrast for all important text.

Rules:

- Body text should usually be Ink Text on white or Frost Blue.
- Blue text must be dark enough to read.
- Do not place pale blue text on pale backgrounds.
- Buttons must pass contrast checks.
- Decorative shapes must not sit behind important text unless very subtle.

## Brand Quality Checklist

Before finalising any page or design asset, check:

- Does it look like VCUK within three seconds?
- Does it use navy, blue, white, and soft blue-grey correctly?
- Is the headline clear and useful?
- Is there one obvious next step?
- Does the design feel custom, not templated?
- Is the page premium without feeling cold?
- Would this build trust with a small business owner?
- Does it avoid generic agency language?
- Does it still feel like the Facebook post and logo belong to the same system?

If the answer is no, refine before building or publishing.
