# VCUK Web Services Redesign Brief

## Project Goal

Redesign VCUK Web Services into a premium, trustworthy, conversion-led website that competes with established Cheshire web agencies while keeping the VCUK edge: clear advice, practical websites, transparent pricing, direct access to Ian, and a strong understanding of small local businesses.

This is not a template site. It must feel designed, written, and built by someone who understands how real small businesses get enquiries.

## Competitive Context

Bright Lemon Websites positions itself as a broader digital agency offering web design, SEO, PPC, and social media. Its strengths are:

- Broad agency offer with multiple services.
- Strong phone/contact visibility.
- Visible recent work.
- Google review proof.
- Clear "we help you get found" language.
- Local Cheshire credibility.

VCUK should not try to look like a smaller Bright Lemon. VCUK should win by feeling more direct, more practical, more transparent, and more personal.

## VCUK Edge

VCUK should own this position:

> Premium, practical websites for local service businesses that are too busy for confusing tech but too good to look basic online.

Supporting ideas:

- Built by Ian, not passed around an agency.
- Clear pricing before a sales call.
- Websites designed around enquiries, not decoration.
- Strong fit for Facebook-first businesses that need one clear home online.
- Honest advice if a smaller fix is enough.
- Fast, mobile-first, SEO-aware builds.
- Plain English without looking cheap.

## Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Local service businesses in Cheshire, Winsford, Northwich, Crewe, Nantwich, Chester, Wilmslow, Macclesfield, and nearby areas.
- Owner-operated businesses using Facebook, Instagram, referrals, or Google Business Profile but lacking a strong website.
- Businesses where trust, photos, reviews, location, and contact speed matter.

Best-fit niches:

- Home improvement and trades.
- Cleaning businesses.
- Pet care and dog services.
- Beauty, grooming, wellness, and clinics.
- Car detailing and vehicle services.
- Local professional services.
- Specialist local businesses with visible proof and good reviews.

Secondary audience:

- Existing small businesses with dated or confusing websites.
- Businesses that need a redesign before SEO or paid ads make sense.
- Businesses wanting ongoing website care but not a big agency retainer.

## Brand Personality

The website should feel:

- Premium but not corporate.
- Clear but not plain.
- Confident but not pushy.
- Local but not small-time.
- Helpful but commercially sharp.
- Human, practical, and direct.

Avoid:

- Generic agency language.
- AI-sounding filler.
- Overused claims such as "we create stunning websites".
- Fake scale.
- Stock-photo business people.
- Template-card layouts that could belong to any web designer.
- Purple gradient tech styling.
- Excessive animations that slow the site or feel gimmicky.

## Visual Direction

### Overall Feel

Use a polished editorial/service-business style rather than a startup SaaS look. The site should feel like a premium local workshop: crafted, confident, and useful.

The design should show that VCUK can create websites with taste, structure, and commercial thinking.

### Layout Principles

- Mobile-first, but desktop should feel considered and spacious.
- First viewport must immediately communicate the offer, location/market, pricing entry point, and proof direction.
- Use strong content sections, not endless floating cards.
- Create rhythm through alternating full-width bands, split editorial layouts, proof panels, and case-study previews.
- Use real screenshots, browser frames, audits, before/after website structures, and visual proof instead of abstract blobs.
- Keep navigation simple and obvious.

### Suggested Visual Assets

Create or source:

- A premium VCUK website screenshot/mockup in the hero.
- A "Facebook page to clear website" transformation visual.
- Case study screenshots for Playful Paws and labelled concept builds.
- Ian portrait or workspace image if available.
- Local business proof visuals: service sections, quote buttons, review blocks, map/contact panels.
- Guide thumbnails that feel editorial, not AI-generated.

Do not use unrelated stock photos as primary visuals. If imagery is used, it must match the service being discussed.

## Colour and Typography Direction

Use the existing VCUK logo as the anchor, but develop a more premium design system around it.

Recommended palette direction:

- Deep ink/navy or charcoal for authority.
- Clean white/off-white for readability.
- One confident VCUK accent colour drawn from the logo.
- Muted support tones for section backgrounds.
- High contrast for buttons and calls to action.

Avoid:

- Purple-blue gradient agency styling.
- Beige/tan "lifestyle brand" styling.
- Too many accent colours.
- Low-contrast grey text.

Typography:

- Use a strong, readable sans-serif for UI and body.
- Consider a distinctive display typeface for headlines if performance allows.
- Keep copy large enough to read easily on mobile.
- Avoid oversized hero text that pushes proof and CTAs below the fold.

### Pricing Page

Purpose: Build trust through transparency.

Must include:

- Clear "from" prices.
- What affects price.
- What is included and excluded.
- Payment/deposit expectations if known.
- Scope boundaries.
- FAQs on ownership, hosting, updates, domains, and cancellation.

Pricing should avoid looking cheap. Frame £350 as focused and lean, not low-quality.

### Work Page

Purpose: Provide proof even while the portfolio is small.

Must include:

- Real client work first.
- Labelled concept builds, clearly marked as examples.
- Short case-study format:
  - Business type.
  - Challenge.
  - Website approach.
  - Sections included.
  - Outcome or intended outcome.

Never pretend concept work is live client work.

### Guides Page

Purpose: Build authority and support outreach.

Must include:

- Guide cards with category, title, plain-English summary, and reading time.
- Search-friendly intro.
- Internal links from guides to relevant services/pricing/contact.
- Weekly publishing cadence.

### About Page

Purpose: Make Ian trustworthy.

Must include:

- Real personal introduction.
- Why VCUK exists.
- Who VCUK is best for.
- What Ian believes small business websites should do.
- Clear reassurance around honesty, scope, and direct communication.

Avoid overclaiming agency size.

### Contact Page

Purpose: Reduce friction.

Must include:

- Email.

## Voice and Copy Rules

Use:

- Plain English.
- Specific business outcomes.
- Short paragraphs.
- Concrete examples.
- Honest scope.
- Direct but warm CTAs.

Avoid:

- "Unlock your potential."
- "Take your business to the next level."
- "Bespoke digital solutions."
- "Stunning websites."
- "We are passionate about..."
- Any copy that could appear on 500 other web agency sites.

Preferred language:

- "Make enquiries easier."
- "One clear place for customers to understand what you do."
- "Clear services, proof, and contact routes."
- "Designed around how customers choose."
- "No confusing tech talk."
- "A practical next step."

## Success Measures

The redesign is successful if:

- Visitors understand what VCUK does within 5 seconds.
- The site feels more premium than the current version.
- The offer is clearer than a generic web agency.
- Pricing builds trust rather than cheapening the brand.
- Work examples make the quality visible.
- Contact routes are obvious on mobile.
- The site supports outreach by giving prospects something credible to check.
- Content feels human and specific, not AI-written.
